# ml-track-reconstruction

Abstract:

As technology in particle physics advances, the efficiency and accuracy of the software must also improve. Recent research in machine learning is rapidly increasing and might prove useful for particle track identification in the detectors at LHC. Although machine learning offers several advantages, it is relatively new and research is minimal. This paper explores a series of two convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for track identification. The first CNN determines how many tracks are present in the current event, and the second CNN determines which hit points belong together to form a track. The track hit points and noise hits were organised in a 2D and 3D grid and were used to train the models. The first CNN performed extremely accurate in both dimensions. The second network in 2D achieved an accuracy of approximately 84 percent and around 98 percent in the 3D version, depending on the parameters such as the kernel size and number of training events. This paper showed promising results for CNNs being used in particle track reconstruction,  however, there are significant limitations to the models as of now, preventing definitive conclusions about the usage of CNNs in particle physics. The need to increase the efficiency of the track reconstruction process for the LHC is of utmost importance because the current reconstruction process requires the most computational power and time of any experiment in particle studies.
